When it comes to roast beef, the received wisdom is that it should always be cooked on the bone – whether it's a sirloin joint or rib roast – as the bone both conducts heat and adds flavour. However, this doesn’t suit everyone and some of our most popular recipes are bone-free and much easier to carve. Buy what suits you best. More important than the bone is the fat – don’t empted to trim it off as it will baste your meat while it cooks. You can always cut it away when you serve it.
